Elephant
The White Stripes — 2003
Listen: Elephant — The White Stripes

The opening riff of "Seven Nation Army" comes from a guitar run through an octave pedal — not a bass, though the difference barely matters by the time it hits you. Jack White recorded the whole album at Toe Rag Studios in London using gear from the 1960s, no computers, eight days. Meg White's drumming sounds simple because it is; it's also the reason the record hits like a wall. Twenty-three years later, that riff is still being chanted at stadiums by people who couldn't name the album.
