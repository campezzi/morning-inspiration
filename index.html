1984 Monaco Grand Prix — Ayrton Senna
1984 Monaco Grand Prix
Ayrton Senna, 1984

Thirty-one laps into the 1984 Monaco Grand Prix, Ayrton Senna — twenty-four years old, driving an uncompetitive Toleman — was hunting down Alain Prost's McLaren through rain so heavy that drivers could barely see the barriers. In the final laps before the stoppage, his times were running faster than anyone else on the circuit. When race director Jacky Ickx stopped the race before Senna could take the lead, the controversy never quite died: sporting caution or politics, nobody was fully convinced. What remains is footage of a young man in machinery that shouldn't have been competitive doing something that looked less like racing and more like a statement of fact.
