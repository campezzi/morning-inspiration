St Peter's Church, Klippan
Sigurd Lewerentz — 1966

Lewerentz was in his late seventies when he took the commission, and he gave the bricklayers one rule: never cut a brick. Whole units only, so the mortar joints swell and buckle wherever a course doesn't quite land, and the friction of that decision is in every wall. Light comes from a scatter of small openings and settles on a clinker floor that tilts, almost imperceptibly, toward a drain near the font. The nave holds its dark the way a room holds a held breath. Nothing here was built to impress from across a square — it waits for you to stand still in it.
