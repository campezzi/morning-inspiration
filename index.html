Fra Mauro Map
Fra Mauro — 1459

Fra Mauro drew this in a monastery workshop on Murano, a circle eight feet across, filling every margin around it with careful annotation in a hand that never seems to hurry. He trusted an eyewitness over a philosopher — where Ptolemy and the merchants who'd actually made the journey disagreed, the merchants usually won. Off an island in the Indian Ocean he noted a lake said to turn iron into gold, then added that he didn't believe a word of it, keeping the story only "to do justice to the testimony of many people." The finished map was sent to Portugal in 1459; Fra Mauro died the following year while copying it a second time for Venice, and Andrea Bianco finished the copy that still hangs there. He left Jerusalem off-center and Paradise off the map entirely — no one had done that before him.
