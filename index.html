Rain Dogs
Tom Waits — 1985
Listen: Rain Dogs — Tom Waits

The concertina that opens Rain Dogs sounds like it was stolen from a burning building. Tom Waits built this record from squeeze-box, marimba, Keith Richards on guitar, and drums that sound like garbage cans overturning — all of it recorded in New York while the city was still dangerous enough to mean something. The title comes from the homeless who lose their way when rain washes away the scent trails they use to navigate; every song is one of them. Marc Ribot's guitar tears through "Hang Down Your Head" like broken neon.
