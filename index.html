The Great Emu War
Royal Australian Artillery, 1932

In November 1932, Major G.P.W. Meredith led a squad of Royal Australian Artillery into the Western Australian wheat belt with two Lewis guns and ten thousand rounds. Their opponents: roughly twenty thousand emus destroying crops. The birds scattered under fire, regrouped in smaller clusters, and — per the ornithologist assigned to observe — absorbed bullets with something approaching the invulnerability of tanks. The operation was suspended after twelve days. Parliament was asked twice more to send in the troops; it declined both times.
