Apollo 11 Launch
NASA — 1969

Five F-1 engines fire at once, and 6.2 million pounds of rocket comes off the pad in a wall of flame and steam. This frame is the close one — no safe distance, just the base of the tower disappearing into white heat a half-second after ignition. It took a flame trench built to survive it and 7.6 million pounds of thrust to send three men toward the Moon on July 16, 1969. Nothing in the picture looks controlled. It looks like it barely was.
