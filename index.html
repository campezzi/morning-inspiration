Carta Marina
Olaus Magnus — 1539

The Carta Marina took twelve years to complete and, for a generation, remained the most detailed map of Scandinavia in existence. Olaus Magnus drew it in Roman exile, working from memory and the letters of sailors. At the edges, sea creatures surface and close on ships too small to argue back. Inland: armies moving through winter, deer crossing frozen lakes, a man wrestling a bear. The map is not vague because it lacked instruments — it is insistent, every margin occupied, every coast opinionated.
