Mystery and Melancholy of a Street
Giorgio de Chirico — 1914

A girl spins a hoop up an empty street, reduced to a dark cutout against a wall lit like midday in Turin. Ahead of her a shadow reaches into the frame — long, human-shaped, cast by something outside the painting entirely, and there's no way to know what. De Chirico built the street from two perspectives that refuse to agree, so the ground tilts even though nothing in it looks technically wrong. An open boxcar waits in the shade at the far right, doors open, empty. He painted this during what he called his metaphysical period; nobody in it seems to have noticed anything is missing.
