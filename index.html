The Son of Man
René Magritte — 1964

A man in a grey coat and bowler hat stands before a low stone wall, sea behind him, a sky heaped with cloud. Between your eyes and his face floats a green apple. It doesn't hide the face — you know it's there, directly behind the apple — but it removes it from view all the same. Magritte painted this as a self-portrait. The apple is ordinary.
