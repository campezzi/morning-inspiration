The Mystery and Melancholy of a Street
Giorgio de Chirico, 1914

A girl rolls a hoop down an empty arcade, chasing something ahead of her or running from something behind. At the far corner of the picture, a shadow falls across the ground — sharp, human-shaped, belonging to no one the image shows. De Chirico drew the building's colonnades and the wagon's bulk from perspectives that don't reconcile with each other; the light seems to enter from two different afternoons simultaneously. The hoop is still moving. The shadow holds its position.
