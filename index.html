The Student
Anton Chekhov — 1894
The past, he thought, is linked with the present by an unbroken chain of events flowing one out of another. And it seemed to him that he had just seen both ends of that chain; that when he touched one end the other quivered.
— Anton Chekhov, The Student (1894), trans. Constance Garnett

A divinity student walks home in bitter cold on Good Friday, stops at a widow's fire at the edge of a field, and starts retelling the story of Peter denying Christ by a charcoal blaze — three times, a cock crowing each time. He watches the old woman and her daughter flinch with a grief he didn't put there. Chekhov wrote "The Student" in 1894 and said afterward it was his favorite among his own work — the story where the past and present turned out to be the same fire, still burning.
