Concorde
British Aircraft Corporation / Aérospatiale — 1969

The nose drooped on takeoff to give the pilots forward visibility, then locked into its blade-sharp angle for cruise — a small mechanical detail that gave the whole aircraft something like an expression. At Mach 2, Concorde traveled faster than the Earth rotates; flying west, you could watch the sun reverse course. It carried ninety to a hundred passengers at a time, in seats narrower than a domestic commuter jet, across the Atlantic in roughly three and a half hours. It made its last commercial flight on October 24, 2003, landing at Heathrow while crowds stood on the roof to watch. Nobody has built another one.
