Saturn Devouring His Son
Francisco Goya — 1819

Saturn's eyes are not those of a hungry god — they're wide, white-rimmed, panicked, closer to dread than appetite. Goya painted this directly onto the plaster of his dining room wall, one of fourteen pictures he made in his seventies that nobody was meant to see. The figure grips the half-eaten body like something that might escape. The gray ground gives nothing back.
