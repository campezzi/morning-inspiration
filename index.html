Ferrari 250 GTO
Ferrari — 1962

Thirty-six were built between 1962 and 1964, each one hand-shaped in aluminum at the Scaglietti works in Modena, and Ferrari refused to sell one to any driver it considered inadequate. The 3.0-litre Colombo V12 inside was already fifteen years old at the car's introduction; Ferrari kept refining it rather than replacing it, and it produced enough to win the GT class at Le Mans three years running. The body was formed after wind tunnel testing at the University of Pisa — not designed for beauty, tested for speed — and what came out of that process is the shape most people reach for when they say "beautiful car" and mean it seriously. None of the thirty-six were identical: the coachbuilders made small adjustments as they went, and that variability is part of why each one still holds the room.
