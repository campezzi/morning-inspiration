The Steerage
Alfred Stieglitz — 1907

The lower deck of an Atlantic liner in 1907: a mass of immigrants between iron handrails and a gangway, the frame cut diagonally by the footbridge above. Stieglitz was traveling first class when he walked to the bow and saw this below him. The round straw hat at the center, the hollow of the gangway, the funnel — he ran back for his Graflex. He would later call it the first photograph in which he felt he was doing what painting already knew how to do.
