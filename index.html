Gas
Edward Hopper — 1940

Three Mobilgas pumps stand at attention while a man in a white shirt does something to one of them — the painting doesn't say what. The road goes off to the right and disappears into trees. Hopper sets this all down with complete seriousness: the pumps, the canisters, the cooling light, the man who has turned his back on whoever might be watching. If there's a joke, the painting is keeping it.
