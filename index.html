SR-71 Blackbird
Lockheed Skunk Works — 1964

Flat black and delta-winged, the SR-71 at 85,000 feet was beyond pursuit — too high for fighters, too fast for the missiles that tried. It cruised at Mach 3.2, and the airframe reached over 300°C in sustained flight; panels were deliberately gapped on the ground to allow for expansion. The aircraft leaked fuel until it was hot enough to seal itself. Not one was ever lost to enemy action across its entire operational life. When it was finally retired, nothing was built to replace it.
