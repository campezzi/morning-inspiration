Un Chien Andalou
Luis Buñuel & Salvador Dalí — 1929
Watch: Un Chien Andalou — Buñuel & Dalí

The film opens on a cloudless night sky, then a razor, then an eye. Buñuel and Dalí wrote the screenplay in a week from competing nightmares, keeping only what neither of them could explain. A palm crawls with ants. A striped box appears and gets dragged across the floor for no reason the scene supplies. It's sixteen minutes long and has been disturbing people since 1929 without ever clarifying what it disturbed them about.
