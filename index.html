Watch: Fallen Angels — Wong Kar-wai
Fallen Angels
Wong Kar-wai, 1995

Shot almost entirely with a 6.8mm wide-angle lens, the film warps Hong Kong into a city of curved walls and stretched faces, every interior closing in on its characters like a hand around a glass. A hitman and his handler communicate through a jukebox and a vacant apartment. A mute ex-con breaks into closed shops at night and forces strangers to buy things — ice cream, raw pork, haircuts — performing commerce as a kind of desperate intimacy. Christopher Doyle's camera never sits still, and the neon bleeds across the frame the way a memory bleeds into the one next to it. It was originally conceived as a third story in Chungking Express, and you can feel that — the same city, the same loneliness, but colder now, and faster, and less sure anyone is listening.
