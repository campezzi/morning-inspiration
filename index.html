Listen: The Mercy Seat — Nick Cave & the Bad Seeds
The Mercy Seat
Nick Cave & the Bad Seeds, 1988

The song opens with a steady drum beat and a wiry guitar figure, Cave's voice flat and precise as he narrates a death-row inmate's final hours. The chair is described in specific, almost technical terms — the wires, the current, the leather straps. As the verses accumulate, the music doesn't so much build as thicken; by the end the whole band has locked into a single cyclical groove, and Cave is repeating and I'm not afraid to die until the phrase loses its meaning and finds another. It was released on Tender Prey in 1988, and nothing about it has aged.
