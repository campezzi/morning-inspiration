The Cadaver Synod
Pope Stephen VI — 897

In January 897, Pope Stephen VI had the corpse of his predecessor Formosus exhumed, dressed in full papal vestments, and propped on a throne. A deacon was appointed to speak in the dead man's defense against charges that he had held two bishoprics at once. Stephen reportedly screamed at the body during the proceedings. The corpse was found guilty: three blessing fingers were cut from the right hand, the vestments stripped, and the remains thrown into the Tiber. Stephen was deposed and strangled before the year was out.
