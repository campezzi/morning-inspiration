Little Nemo in Slumberland
Winsor McCay — 1905

Each strip ends the same way: Nemo falls out of bed and a parent appears to close the dream. Everything between the opening panel and that last panel is Winsor McCay doing whatever he felt like. In this September 1907 page, Nemo and the Imp have grown tall enough to wade through skyscrapers, the city spreading out at their feet like a dropped toy. McCay built his panels around the figures, not the other way around — rooms that couldn't hold their shape, staircases that tip and stretch and refuse to behave. He launched the strip in the New York Herald in 1905; by 1907 it had the confidence of someone who had realized that comics could get away with anything.
