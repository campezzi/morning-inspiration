Super Pac-Man
Namco — 1982

Namco built Super Pac-Man just a year after the original made it the most famous game on the planet, and the fix nobody asked for was to make the hero bigger. Dots are gone — you eat keys now, unlocking doors into rooms stuffed with fruit, and a power pellet turns Pac-Man into a lumbering giant while the ghosts go thin and flat to sell the illusion of him flying over them. By May of 1983 it was outearning every other machine on the arcade floor. Toru Iwatani, who designed the original, looked at his own sequel and called it boring.
