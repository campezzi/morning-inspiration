Chuck Yeager — 1947

Two nights before the flight, Chuck Yeager fell off a horse and cracked two ribs on his right side. He had a civilian doctor in the nearby town tape them up and told no one at the base who could ground him. On the morning of October 14, 1947, his arm couldn't reach high enough to seal the Bell X-1's hatch, so his engineer sawed the end off a broom handle for him to use as a lever. The plane dropped from the belly of a B-29 at 20,000 feet, lit its rocket engines, and at Mach 1.06 the shockwave that people on the ground heard as a boom never reached the man who caused it. He'd named the aircraft after his wife.

Source: National Air and Space Museum
