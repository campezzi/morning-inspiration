2000 Years With Carl Reiner & Mel Brooks
Mel Brooks & Carl Reiner — 1960
Listen: 2000 Years With Carl Reiner & Mel Brooks — Mel Brooks & Carl Reiner

Carl Reiner interviews a man who claims to be two thousand years old, and Mel Brooks never breaks character once, answering in a wheedling old-man drawl that sounds like it's been doing this bit since Rome. There was no script — Reiner started carrying a tape recorder to parties because Brooks never told the same story twice. Asked about Jesus, the old man remembers him fondly: "thin lad, wore sandals, long hair, walked around with eleven other guys. Never bought anything. Always asked for water." Steve Allen was the one who finally talked them into a studio, in 1960, to get it down on record before it disappeared. It's been sitting in the Library of Congress since 2008, filed a few shelves over from the national anthem.
