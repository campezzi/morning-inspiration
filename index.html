Pale Blue Dot
NASA / Voyager 1 — 1990

On February 14, 1990, with its primary mission complete, Voyager 1 turned its camera back toward the sun. Earth appears in the frame as a fragment of light — 0.12 pixels, caught in a ray of scattered sunlight from the camera's optics. The photograph was Carl Sagan's idea; he spent years persuading NASA to authorize the maneuver, arguing there would be only one chance. The distance was about 6 billion kilometres. The camera was switched off soon after.
