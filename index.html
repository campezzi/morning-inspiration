Automat
Edward Hopper — 1927

A woman sits alone at a marble-topped table in an all-night automat, one glove off, one still on, a full cup of coffee cooling in front of her. Behind her the window holds nothing but two rows of ceiling lights doubling into the dark — no street, no reflection of the room, just black glass going back forever. Hopper painted it in 1927 and unveiled it that Valentine's Day, though there's nothing sentimental in it: she doesn't look up, and nothing in the picture suggests she will. The bowl of fruit at the table's edge is the only warm color in the room, and it doesn't help.
