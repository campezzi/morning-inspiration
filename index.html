Netherlandish Proverbs
Pieter Bruegel the Elder — 1559

Bruegel fits over a hundred Flemish proverbs into a single village — not to celebrate folk wisdom but to catalogue what people reliably do. One man bangs his head against a wall. Another fishes behind the net. In the center, a woman drapes a blue cloak over her husband, which was the painting's original title: The Blue Cloak, or the Fool's Cloak. Every figure is occupied; not one appears surprised.
