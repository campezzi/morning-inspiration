Giant Steps
John Coltrane — 1960
Listen: Giant Steps — John Coltrane

The harmonic architecture of the title track cycles through three key centers a major third apart, so fast that the pianist Tommy Flanagan stumbles mid-solo on the recording — caught between changes in real time, the stumble left in. Coltrane had worked it all out on paper before the session, but knowing the logic didn't make the tempo merciful. Every track on the album moves like that: forward, never looking back, the rhythm section barely keeping contact. Recorded in 1959 and released the following year, it still sounds like something chasing its own tail — not anxious, just constitutionally unable to sit still.
