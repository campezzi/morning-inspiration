Mean Streets
Martin Scorsese — 1973

Charlie walks into Tony's bar in slow motion to "Be My Baby" and the film never really slows back down after that. Scorsese shoots Little Italy handheld and close, jukebox needle drops standing in for a score, the camera trailing Keitel and De Niro like it's trying to keep up with them rather than direct them. Johnny Boy owes money he can't pay and won't stop talking; Charlie keeps promising he'll fix it. Nothing here waits for you to catch your breath.
