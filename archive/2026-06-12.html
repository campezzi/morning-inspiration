1976 Italian Grand Prix
Niki Lauda — 1976
Watch: 1976 Italian Grand Prix — Niki Lauda

Forty-two days after being pulled from a burning Ferrari at the Nürburgring and read last rites at the hospital, Niki Lauda arrived at Monza and drove. The gauze on his scalp was still there; he bled into his helmet during practice. He qualified sixth and finished fourth. At the season's final round at Fuji — rain, near-zero visibility, standing water — he came in after two laps and said the conditions weren't worth dying for. His rival James Hunt took the title by a single point.
