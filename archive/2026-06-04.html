Marriage A-la-Mode: The Tête à Tête
William Hogarth — 1743

The husband has slumped in his armchair in last night's clothes; at the far end of the room his wife sits in mid-stretch, a playing card on the floor beside her. A small dog is already at his coat pocket, pulling out what appears to be a woman's cap. By the door, a servant stacks unpaid bills without urgency; the steward behind him takes his leave, ledger under arm, with the expression of someone who has seen this before and will not be back. Hogarth painted five more scenes in this series, each one a step further down.
