Carceri d'Invenzione
Giovanni Battista Piranesi — 1750

The staircases in Piranesi's imaginary prisons go up, arrive somewhere, and keep going. He began the series at twenty-five — fever work, reportedly — then revised all sixteen plates in 1761, deepening the shadows and adding chains whose purpose the architecture refuses to explain. The figures scattered through the vaults are small enough to be lost. No cell is visible, no door: what imprisons them is the space itself, which goes on.
