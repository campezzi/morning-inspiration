Therme Vals
Peter Zumthor — 1996

Water moves through the building in a series of chambers, each one a different temperature, a different dark. Zumthor used 60,000 slabs of local quartzite — cut from the same mountain that surrounds Vals — so the bath feels less like a building entered than a hillside hollowed. The roof floats on a seam of glass above the stone walls; a thin stripe of daylight at the joint. In the hot dark, the body stops keeping time.
