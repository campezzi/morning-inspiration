Isle of the Dead
Arnold Böcklin — 1880

Böcklin called it his Bild zum Träumen — "a painting to dream by" — which is either the most honest thing a painter ever said about his work or the most unsettling. The island has no name on any map. The cypress trees grow too densely for a real place, too deliberately for a painted one. A white-robed figure stands in the bow of the skiff, back to you, and you can't decide whether it's arriving or leaving, or what moves in the water beneath the oar. Böcklin painted five versions between 1880 and 1886, each slightly different, as if he kept trying to get the island to hold still.
