Shadows
John Cassavetes — 1959

Three siblings drift through late-1950s Manhattan — a jazz trumpeter, his younger brother losing himself in the city, their sister caught up in a romance that turns on race. Cassavetes shot it on the streets with a 16mm camera and no fixed script. It moves the way its jazz soundtrack moves: loosely, room for the unexpected beat. A party scene goes nowhere; a fight simmers without landing. The whole film keeps moving like something is about to happen that never quite does.
