Scenographia Systematis Copernicani
Andreas Cellarius — 1660

The Sun sits at the center, and the planets wheel around it in labeled orbits — Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn — arranged in concentric bands. Cellarius published this plate in Amsterdam in 1660, nearly a century after Copernicus proposed the arrangement it depicts; in the four corners, allegorical figures look inward at a model the world was still debating. The hand-coloring is careful and deliberate, each planetary band filled in a different hue. At the outer edge, a thin ring of fixed stars marks the limit of what the atlas claims to know.
