The Devil's Dictionary
Ambrose Bierce — 1906

BORE, n. A person who talks when you wish him to listen.

FUTURE, n. That period of time in which our affairs prosper, our friends are true and our happiness is assured.

HISTORY, n. An account mostly false, of events mostly unimportant, which are brought about by rulers mostly knaves, and soldiers mostly fools.

— Ambrose Bierce, The Devil's Dictionary (1906)

The Devil's Dictionary defines a bore as "a person who talks when you wish him to listen," and continues in that register for several hundred more entries without softening. Bierce assembled it over decades of newspaper columns, apparently without any interest in whether readers agreed. It appeared in book form in 1906 under the title The Cynic's Word Book; the more honest name came later, when his collected works went to press. In 1913, at seventy-one, he rode into Mexico to observe Pancho Villa's revolution, sent one last letter, and was never found.
