The Critic
Weegee — 1943

Two women glide toward the Metropolitan Opera's opening night in 1943, furs on, one in a tiara, and just over their shoulders a third woman turns to look — mouth pinched shut, eyes narrowed into a glare that needs no caption. Weegee staged the whole thing: he'd spotted her drinking at a bar on the Bowery, walked her over, and waited with his flash for the moment her face curdled. Life ran it first under the title "The Fashionable People." Two years later he renamed it "The Critic" for his book Naked City, and the new title did what the old one couldn't — told you exactly whose side the photograph was on.
