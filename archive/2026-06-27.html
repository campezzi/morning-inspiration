À bout de souffle
Jean-Luc Godard — 1960

Michel Poiccard steals a car in Marseille, shoots a highway cop without breaking stride, and drifts into Paris to collect a debt that never materializes. Godard shot the street scenes with the camera hidden inside a postal cart — the city had no idea. The jump cuts weren't a plan; they came from stripping dead time out of longer takes until the film moved the way Michel moved: no pause, no destination, forward in the wrong direction. Jean Seberg sells the Herald Tribune on the Champs-Élysées and has the last word.
