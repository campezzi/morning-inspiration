The Third-Class Carriage
Honoré Daumier — 1864

Third-class meant wooden benches and no real view, and the people in Daumier's carriage have made their peace with this. A grandmother holds her basket steady in her lap; a mother beside her nurses without looking at the child; a boy next to them sits with the stillness of someone accustomed to waiting. Daumier left the painting unfinished. In first class, there were cushions.
