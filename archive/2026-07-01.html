Allie Mae Burroughs
Walker Evans — 1936

She stands against the bare clapboard of her house in Hale County, Alabama, hair pulled back tight, mouth set somewhere between rest and effort. Walker Evans made the photograph in the summer of 1936, working for the Farm Security Administration alongside the writer James Agee, who lived with her family for eight weeks. Nothing in the frame asks for sympathy — not the cracked wood behind her, not the collar of her dress, buttoned to the throat. Her name was Allie Mae Burroughs. She sat for Evans again decades later, in 1979, with the same flat, unhurried gaze.
