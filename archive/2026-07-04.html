Fulcrum
Richard Serra — 1987

Five plates of Cor-Ten steel, tilted against each other to a height of seventeen meters, rise just outside Liverpool Street station — taller than most of the office towers that came up around it since. No single plate stands on its own; each one leans on its neighbor, the whole structure held up by mutual weight and nothing else. The steel has gone the deep orange-brown of a decades-old rust bloom, streaked where rain runs down the flanks. When the site above the station was redeveloped a few years ago, engineers didn't relocate it — they lowered the whole sculpture a meter and a half and left it standing exactly where Serra put it in 1987.
