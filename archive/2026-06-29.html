The Elephant Celebes
Max Ernst — 1921

The creature at the centre of this painting is not quite an elephant. Ernst based the form on a photograph of a Sudanese corn bin — ribbed, cylindrical, sealed — and planted it in a flat, ambiguous space between ground and sky. A fish-tail curves from one end; a metal head with bull horns tilts where no head should be. To the right, a headless nude in a white glove gestures toward it, or away — it's hard to say which. The painting was made in 1921, and whatever it is looking at, it has not blinked since.
