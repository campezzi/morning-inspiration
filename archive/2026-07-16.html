Music Has the Right to Children
Boards of Canada — 1998
Listen: Music Has the Right to Children — Boards of Canada

Boards of Canada made this record in a home studio called Hexagon Sun, in the Pentland Hills outside Edinburgh, on tape machines and synths they'd deliberately detuned. While recording "Rue the Whirl" someone left the window open, and birdsong bled onto the tape; they kept it in. Other tracks sample old Sesame Street recordings — a child's voice, slowed and warped until it stops sounding like language and starts sounding like a memory of language. The cover is a family photograph taken at Banff Springs, altered until the faces are barely there. Nothing on the album resolves; it just keeps fading in the direction it was already fading.
