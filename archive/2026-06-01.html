Olivetti Valentine
Ettore Sottsass — 1969

The red one — there were other colors, but the red became the image. Sottsass had borrowed his palette from a Tom Wesselmann painting; he wanted the Valentine to feel like that: pop, deliberate, slightly confrontational about where you were allowed to write. It came with a flat cardboard carry sleeve and was marketed, sincerely, as a typewriter for anywhere but the office. The case was engineered to double as a stool. If you find one in good condition, it still types beautifully.
