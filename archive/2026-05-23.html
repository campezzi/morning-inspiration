Little Nemo in Slumberland
Winsor McCay — 1905

Every Sunday, Nemo tumbled through a different impossible country before landing on the bedroom floor in the final panel. Winsor McCay built these dreams as full-page architectural extravaganzas — staircases that doubled back on themselves, columns that grew legs, giants who leaned down with perfect courtesy. In this strip, a princess big enough to hold the whole block in one hand reaches in to say hello, and McCay gives her a polite incline of the head. Slumberland is enormous and extremely well-mannered. The fall out of bed happens every week, but you always want one more panel.
