Brion Cemetery
Carlo Scarpa — 1978

At the propylaeum, two rings overlap in blue and pink mosaic — the vesica piscis, Scarpa's device for the union of husband and wife, framing the garden beyond before the path reaches the tomb itself. Giuseppe and Onorina Brion lie side by side under a raw concrete arcosolium, inclined toward each other, in the spot that catches the most sun anywhere on the site. Scarpa worked the commission from 1968 until his death in 1978, folding in Japanese joinery and Byzantine gold tesserae, and a stepped, ziggurat-like edge that recurs at nearly every threshold and corner. He's buried here too, just outside the boundary wall, standing upright and wrapped in linen like a medieval knight rather than laid in a coffin. A reflecting pool covers much of the ground between the chapel and the meditation pavilion, and the water is the only thing that moves.
