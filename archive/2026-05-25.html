Spiral Jetty
Robert Smithson — 1970

Robert Smithson coiled 6,650 tons of black basalt and earth into a 1,500-foot spiral in the shallows of Great Salt Lake in April 1970. The lake can rise high enough to submerge the whole thing; it has. It also retreats, leaving the rocks salt-white and sun-cracked, and the spiral reappears. Smithson died in a plane crash three years later while scouting another site in Texas. The jetty is still there, no one tends it, and it doesn't need them to.
