The Dancing Plague of 1518
Frau Troffea — 1518

On a July morning in 1518, a woman named Frau Troffea stepped into a Strasbourg street and began to dance. She didn't stop for six days. Within a month, roughly four hundred people had joined her, dancing in the summer heat until their feet bled and some collapsed dead from exhaustion. The physicians of Strasbourg, diagnosing the cause as hot blood, prescribed more dancing; the city accordingly hired musicians and built a stage. It did not help.
