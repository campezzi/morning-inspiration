Lamborghini Miura
Marcello Gandini / Bertone — 1966

The Miura appeared at the 1966 Geneva Motor Show looking like something that had escaped the Mille Miglia and didn't particularly want to be caught. Marcello Gandini was twenty-six when he designed it — his first major project at Bertone — and he gave it chrome-lashed headlights and louvred rear haunches as if a sports car had no business being demure about any of this. The V12 is mounted transversely behind the seats, an arrangement borrowed from single-seater racing that nobody had tried in a road car; the whole assembly sits so close to the cockpit that the car runs warm by nature. Lamborghini named it for a ranch in Andalucía that bred fighting bulls, which tells you exactly what kind of car it thought it was.
