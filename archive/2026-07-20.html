Porsche 917K
Porsche — 1970

The tube-frame chassis weighed about 42 kilograms — so light that Porsche pressurized it with gas after welding and watched the gauge for any drop that would flag a hairline crack in a weld. The shape wasn't right at first: the original long-tailed 917 was so unstable at speed that a car crashed fatally in the opening minutes of its debut race, Le Mans 1969, and Porsche spent the following year in the wind tunnel cutting the tail down into the short "Kurzheck" body with twin stabilizing fins. In John Wyer's pale blue and marigold Gulf livery, the shortened car won the Daytona 24 Hours in 1970, then became one of motorsport's most recognizable liveries when it appeared on-screen in Steve McQueen's film Le Mans the following year. Fully assembled it weighed just over 800 kilograms — some 150 to 200 kilograms lighter than its Ferrari rivals.
