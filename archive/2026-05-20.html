Earthrise
William Anders — 1968

The Earth holds itself above the lunar horizon — half-lit, blue-white, improbably vivid against the black. Frank Borman spotted it first and called out; William Anders grabbed the camera and loaded color film in the rush. It was Christmas Eve, 1968, and Apollo 8 had been in lunar orbit less than four hours. The shot had no planned place in the mission; the astronauts were supposed to be photographing the surface below. Looking at it now, the grey foreground — pocked, airless, silent — doesn't diminish the Earth so much as give it somewhere to be.
