Brion Vega Cemetery
Carlo Scarpa — 1978

The Brion family cemetery in San Vito d'Altivole begins with a low concrete wall enclosing the municipal graveyard on two sides, its perimeter fitted with a continuous diamond-cut groove that runs the whole length — an edge that tells you, before you've gone in, that every surface has been attended to. At the center of the complex, two concrete arches lean toward each other above the family tomb, almost but not quite meeting. Water appears throughout in shallow channels and in a pool where a meditation pavilion floats. Scarpa worked here for nine years; when he died in November 1978 he was buried inside the outer wall, at a place he had chosen, which faces north.
